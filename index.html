What is the accrued interest?
The accrued interest is the interest that has been incurred as of a specific date on a loan or any other liability that isnot paid yet. How to calculate accrued interest?
To calculate accrued interest you should use the following formula:
accrued interest = loan amount * (yearly interest / 365) * accrued interest period
example: company x took a loan and the amount of the loan was $13,500, yearly interest was 12% and the accrued interest period was 90 days, calculate the accrued interest?
Using the accrued interest formula the answer will be:
accrued interest = loan amount * (yearly interest / 365) * accrued interest period
accrued interest = $13,500 * (0.12 / 365) * 90
accrued interest = $399.45
Why use accrued interest?
the accrued interest is used to account for the interest that has accrued over a period of time without being paid yet.
